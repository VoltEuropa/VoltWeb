Become a Member

Are you interested in joining us and become member ? Below please find some information on the process and what you can expect when joining us.

How do I become a member?

Becoming member means joining our european community with membership in our national chapter Volt France as well as our European Association (Volt Europa).

We have a European-wide membership portal, called, where you can register and apply for membership:

Go to volt.team

What does it mean to be a Member?

We are a volunteer organisation working on European, national (, regional) and local level. There are more than enough things to do and responsabilities to take on all levels, both administrative and functional. For example developing and contributing to policy topics that interest you, organising local events and participating in other associations, making our IT systems more robust and open source or interviewing and onboarding new members. We have a relatively flat hierarchy, a buddy system to show new members around in our online workplace as well as regular and introductory online calls. After that, you are free to pick your interests, start contributing and hopefully learn how to organise a community and getting more people back involved in politics.

There is no written requirement but we are hoping that members can make themselves available a few hours per week to work on projects they care for.

In legal terms, being a member means you have a voting right on all member votes being held on your local, national and the european level. Votes include for example whether to accept or reject a new policy, administrative votes for regional representatives and members of the board, which group our elected MEP in Germany should join in the European Parliament or simply the city our next general convention should be held in. As a member you can of course also declare yourself a candidate for every post available.

We are in the process of setting up membership fees which will form the future base of having a right to vote. Until the system is setup, all official members can participate in votes.

What if I am already a member of another political party or if I don't want to become a member?

Volt is an open movement. We care more for issues than political colors, which means you are welcome to join one of our local teams and work with us for as long as you like. We are glad and appreciate the time anyone is willing to give towards improving our community and society as a whole and for us, every contribution counts. If at some point you feel like becoming an official member, you will however have to do so without any other active party membership.

What steps are involved in becoming a member?

After registering on volt.team you will be contacted by our HR team for scheduling an interview. Please have a bit of patience until we get back to you - our HR team is also made up of volunteers and we're going through applications one by one.
